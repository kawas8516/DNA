# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences:
1. **Gamers, developers, and artists** — online-native creatives looking for a community where they can build together. They want energy, belonging, and the sense that something real is happening here.
2. **Potential business partners** — brands and clients evaluating DNA for influencer marketing, community management, or software design. They want credibility, ambition, and signs of traction.

The page must speak to both without splitting into two voices: the community energy IS the credibility signal.

## Product Purpose

Discord Nation Alpha is a community-first creative collective. The site's job is to communicate what DNA is and make both audiences want in — whether as a member or a collaborator. Success is a visitor who says "this feels different" and acts on it (joins, reaches out, follows).

## Brand Personality

Bold, Creative, Inclusive. Energetic without being loud. Community-native without being informal. Ambitious without being corporate.

Reference: Discord — playful, approachable, colorful, conversational but not childish.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup**: gradient heroes, metric cards, "Move fast" copy on clean white. Feels like every other Y Combinator landing page.
- **Dark gamer aesthetic**: neon on black, aggressive angles, RGB vibes. Feels like a peripheral brand, not a people-first community.

## Design Principles

1. **The community IS the product.** Show people, energy, and collaboration — not abstract value propositions. Real content over polished copy.
2. **Bold but human.** Take visual risks (color, scale, motion) but always ground them in warmth and accessibility, not spectacle.
3. **One voice, two readers.** The page speaks once; each audience reads what they need. Don't fork the narrative.
4. **Earned simplicity.** Whitespace should feel like breathing room, not absence. Only strip back when what remains is stronger.
5. **Motion serves memory.** Animations earn their place by making the brand feel alive, not by decorating scroll events.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Community brand — broad, inclusive audience. Reduced motion must be respected. Color contrast checked at all text sizes.
